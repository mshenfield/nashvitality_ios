Bike Share: a service in which bicycles are made available for shared use by individuals. This service includes as a series of locations that provide free or affordable access to bicycles for short-distance trips in an urban area as an alternative to motorized public transportation or private vehicles or provide leisurely rides along parks and greenways. Helmets are not provided. The use of a personal helmet is highly recommended.

Bike Parking: publically available bike racks for storing or securing bicycles. Locks are not provided.

Community Center: a facility operated by the Metro Parks Department that provides community gathering space and a wide range of programs and services. These options may include a gym, fitness facilities, exercises classes, and organized sports.

Dog Park: a facility set aside for dogs to exercise and play off-leash in a controlled environment under the supervision of their owners.

EV Charging Station: Electric Vehicle station is an element in an infrastructure that supplies electric energy for the recharging of plug-in electric vehicles, including all-electric cars, neighborhood electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids.

Groove: an innovative map of easy-riding routes across Nashville that interconnect neighborhoods and highlights comfortable cycling for all level riders.

LEED: stands for Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design, consists of a suite of rating systems for the design, construction and operation of high performance green buildings, homes and neighborhoods. As an internationally recognized mark of excellence, LEED provides building owners and operators with a framework for identifying and implementing practical and measurable green building design, construction, operations and maintenance solutions.

Low Impact Development: an approach to land development (or re-development) that works with nature to manage stormwater as close to its source as possible. Elements may include bioretention, rain gardens, green roofs, rain harvesting, pervious pavement and infiltration trench.

Metro Greenway: Greenways are linear parks with paved multi-use trails primarily along Nashville’s river corridors which connect neighborhoods to schools, shopping areas, downtown, offices, recreation areas, open spaces and other points of activity. Often located along natural landscape features like streams, rivers and ridges, or along built features, such as railroad corridors and scenic highways, greenways provide valuable greenspace for conservation, recreation and alternative transportation

Mountain Bike trail: off-road dirt path that may have rugged terrain and are best used by bicycles

MTA: stands for Metro Transit Authority. Nashville’s public transportation agency.

Music City Bikeway: a 26-mile bike route that connects eastern and western Davidson County through a continuous path of greenways, park roads, bike paths and shared lanes

Nashville B-Cycle: fee-based, automated kiosk bike share system within Nashville’s urban core designed for quick commuter trips

Nashville GreenBikes: free bike share system for leisure or exercise in parks and greenways

Nature Center: a facility operated by the Metro Parks Department that provides environmental education, helps manage the park ecosystem and foster respect for the natural environment. These centers offer natural history information and literature, maps, special events, field trips, and naturalists to answer questions.

Parks: parks that are owned or maintained by Metro Parks Department, State of Tennessee or the US Corps of Engineers that may include natural areas, open land, recreation space or other features

Paved Trail: an improved surface multi-use path that is a solid, flat surface best used for walking, running, cycling, in-line skating and for pushing strollers

Playground: an outdoor area for recreation and play that contains equipment such as slides and swings

Recycling: Recycling is the process of reusing or reconditioning materials or products so they can be used to make new products.

Recycling Center: A drop-off facility for the recycling of waste paper, bottles, cans, or other materials, including small collection facilities and reverse vending machines.

Retrofit: the improvement of the infrastructure of the building to increase its energy efficiency, comfort, safety, health and durability. This could include improving building components, building operating systems and equipment, and installing energy efficient appliances.

Road Routes: paved paths along city streets that include one or all three of the following:

  • Sharrow: shared roadway bicycle markings to alert other road users to expect bicyclists to occupy travel lanes
  • Designated Bike Lane: separate lanes on city roadways that are designated for cyclists only
  • Wide Outside Lanes: a roadway that it is wide enough to be safely shared side by side by a bicycle and a motor vehicle at the same time

Splash Park: an area in the Metro Park system for water play that includes fountains and other spray features.

Sports Facility: outdoor or indoor space that accommodates athletic activities such as basketball, football, baseball, softball, soccer, tennis, golf, ice skating and other sports

Transit: Mass transit, public transportation system(s) in which passengers are carried in large numbers

Unpaved Trails: a natural surface path that has been cleared and compacted. It may have rocks and rugged terrain and is well suited for hiking and trail running

Walk 100 Miles: a citywide health challenge issued by Mayor Karl Dean to encourage Nashvillians to walk regularly for exercise. The current challenge lasts through the end of 2012. Any participant that walks 100 miles and logs their progress on the walk100miles.com website will receive a free congratulatory t-shirt.

Waste: Material that is not wanted; the unusable remains or byproducts of something

Waterway Access: public and convenient access to rivers that can be used by non-motorized boats, such as canoes or kayaks, throughout the year

Wave Country: a paid-admission water park owned and operated by the Metro Parks Department, including a wave-action swimming pool, three water flumes and two speed slides, a “kiddie” pool. This facility is open in the summer from Memorial Day to Labor Day.
